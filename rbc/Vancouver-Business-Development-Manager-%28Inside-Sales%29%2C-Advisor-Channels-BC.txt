Location:Vancouver, BC, CA
Date Posted:2015-06-16

purpose: 


working in partnership with a district vice president (wholesaler) and sales coordinator, the business development manager’s (inside sales) key responsibility is to prospect and cultivate new relationships, as well as enhance existing relationships with financial advisors and planners in a defined territory with the goal of increasing the sales of rbc gam products.  lead generation comes from mining an existing database of contacts as well as using various websites and resources to uncover new contacts.  


 


responsibilities:


- work actively with the district vice president to establish a strategic business plan for the territory with defined sales targets


- proactively contact financial advisors and planners to gain information and insight into their practices


- provide insight and ideas about capital markets, economics, and investment instrument 


- position rbc, ph&n, and bluebay products and solutions to prospects and existing clients


- advance the sales process with new prospects by booking presentations and appointments for the district vice president


- create and deliver presentations, proposals and investment illustrations as required


 


what we deliver:


- excellent earning potential through a base salary and direct drive commission


- ongoing training, skills development and coaching


- career coaching and advancement opportunities


- constant access to information and opinion on capital markets from portfolio managers, research teams and our chief economist


- annual and semi-annual national sales meeting for additional training and sharing of best practices


- opportunity to spend time in the field with the district vice president to assist in hosting seminars, presentations, client meals, and entertainment events


- a comprehensive “total rewards” package including health benefits, pension, retirement, and investment plans


-to be part of a large and growing organization which puts client interests first and has a culture of fairness and accountability


 


requirements/qualifications:


- university degree or equivalent, preferably in commerce, economics, finance or related


- excellent written, verbal communication skills 


- completion the canadian securities course (csc)


- completion of, or enrolment in an advanced financial services designation such as cim, cfp, pfp, cfa is considered an asset


- experience working in the financial services industry


- excellent knowledge of and genuine interest in capital markets and financial instruments.


 


soft skill requirements:


- genuinely enjoy sales and client interaction


- motivated by results and success


- excellent conversation skills with the ability to quickly establish rapport


- ability to handle rejection and rebuttals.


- aptitude for managing competing priorities and initiatives


- passion for finance and investments


- self-motivated and driven
